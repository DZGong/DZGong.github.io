TACO

Tandem Aberration Correction Optics (TACO) system for high quality super-resolution SIM imaging through strong aberrating medium.

TACO setup

The TACO system is a novel adaptive optics system developed to fully correct all kinds of aberrations in SIM. This system incorporates both conjugate adaptive optics and pupil adaptive optics with distinctively designed algorithms to correct for local aberrations in the illumination path and global aberrations in the detection path separately.

Figure: 2D and 3D schematics of the TACO system setup.

Conjugate AO

The sinusoidal pattern of SIM illumination is known to be "immune" to global aberrations but suffers from local aberrations. TACO optimizes the phase modulation of the SLM at the conjugate plane of the illumination and counteracts the spatially varying distortions of the SIM pattern.

Figure: A typical optimization process of the conjugate AO for local aberrations.

Pupil AO

TACO further utilizes a DM to perform Zernike-polynomial-based pupil AO in the detection path of SIM to correct for all possible global aberrations, such as spherical aberration, coma, astigmatism.

Figure: A typical optimization process of the pupil AO for global aberrations.

TACO-SIM imaging

With the aberration correction by TACO, high quality SIM imaging can be achieved even through strongly aberrating media, allowing for super-resolution imaging in both lateral and axial directions with high fidelity and robustness.

Figure: WF and SIM images of the phalloidin labeled actin in a human melanoma cell.
