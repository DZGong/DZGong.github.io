TACO

Tandem Aberration Correction Optics (TACO) system for high quality super-resolution SIM imaging through strong aberrating medium.

TACO setup

The TACO system is designed for correcting the different types of aberrations of Structured Illumination Microscopy (SIM) separately. TACO incorporates a spatial light modulatior (SLM) for conjugate adaptive optics (AO) in the illumination path to compensate for the long neglecated local (spatial-variant) aberrations in SIM illumination, while a deformable mirror (DM) is implemented in the detection path as a classic pupil adaptive optics (AO) component to correct for global aberrations.

Figure: 2D and 3D schematics of the TACO system setup.

Conjugate AO

The sinusoidal pattern of SIM illumination is known to be "immune" to global aberrations, but suffers from local aberrations. TACO utilizes an stochastic paralleled gradient descent (SPGD) based algorithm to optimize the phase modulation of the SLM at the conjugate plane of the illumination and counteracts the spatially varying distortions of SIM pattern.

Figure: A typical optimization process of the conjugate AO for local aberrations.

Pupil AO

TACO further utilizes a DM to do Zernike-polynomial based pupil AO in the detection path of SIM to correct for all the possible global aberrations such as spherical aberation, coma, astigmatism.

Figure: A typical optimization process of the pupil AO for global aberrations.

TACO-SIM imaging

With the aberration correction by TACO, high quality SIM imaging can be achieved even through strongly aberrating medium, allowing for super-resolution imaging in both lateral and axial directions with high fidelity and robustness.
